    • Tips on Conserving Energy

     

    Hiking is all about having a good time. That's hard to do when you're having trouble when energy levels are low. We recommend eating a large, carbohydrate meal the night before. This will help rejuvenate your energy levels, the body stores unused complex carbohydrates in the liver that's then converted to energy when needed.

    Without a doubt, getting a quality night of sleep prior to your hike will help you remain aware of your surroundings, and aid in an energetic hike.

    Tips on Staying Safe

       

    When it comes to hiking, safety should be your number one priority. Mother nature can be unforgiving, make sure you're prepared.

    Plan your trip with friends, stick together and avoid wandering off alone. In the event of a crisis, having multiple pairs of hands to help is ALWAYS better than one.

    Be sure to triple check your survival/hiking guide to make sure you are bringing everything you need. Be sure to include a first aid along with copious amounts of water.
